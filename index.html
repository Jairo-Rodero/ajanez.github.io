Job Market Paper

Álvaro Jáñez, Lukas Delgado-Prieto
July 2023

Monopsony Power and Firm Organization

Labor market competition for managers and production workers drastically differs in Portugal. Using administrative data together with a general equilibrium model where firms organize production in hierarchies, we show that the heterogeneity in monopsony power leads to a welfare loss of 5.7\% for production workers and 23.1\% for managers. This heterogeneity implies that occupation-based minimum wages are more effective in alleviating monopsony power than a single minimum wage. Recent statutory raises in the minimum wage lower overall welfare. Moving to an optimal occupation-based minimum wage generates a welfare gain of 0.2\% for both occupations.
